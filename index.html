It took me countless tests, practicing, and memorizing to get the rank I currently hold. Taekwondo is not the only martial art I practice; I practice Muay Thai and Jiu Jitsu on my free days. Over Gualo Rai, Saipan, I help my uncle run the Camacho Taekwondo dojo right beside Rocksteady. There are currently two sessions, and my brother and instruct and supervise both classes. Although I am an instructor, I am not able to teach on my own. Intro

The reason for this website is to explain what martial arts is, its purpose, and that all martial arts are not taught the same way. The belt ranking systems are set with requirements for each student to achieve, not to show how skilled each person is at fighting. Black belt ranks are not just the highest rank a person can achieve, but it is also a milestone and proof of hardwork and dedication an individual has given to the art they practice.

Belt Ranking System

Be aware, the belt ranking system is put in place to let others know how long each student has been practicing. The belt rank does not define the combat skill an individual has. Each belt color determines a student's experience and amount of training they have accumulated. The belt color also helps the master find the right training intensity and required skills for each student. Another good example of what belt colors are used for is to determine the tasks that each student is able to do. Not all white belts are skilled enough to perform such difficult movements, such as, a jumping-reverse kick. Lastly, the belt ranking systems vary for each school and their affiliated martial arts style. Each instructor has a different requirement for each belt color to be achieved. For more info on the belt ranking system, visit Way of Martial Arts to get a better understanding. Or, you can watch the video provided right underneath.

What is a Black Belt?

A black belt is a distinguised rank that signifies the time and dedication an individual has put into the martial art they practice. One common stereotype is that black belts are dangerous, intimidating, and are most likely unbeatable. As a black belt, I can confirm that these stereotypes are very untrue. Most of the black belts I meet are kind, calm, and have a good attitude. Us black belts are almost always the janitors of the dojo. The reason we are told to clean up after the class is to remind ourselves that although we hold high ranks, we are still not as high as our master and need our master to guide us further.

The Purpose of Martial Arts

Martial arts was not meant to inflict such damage, rather, it is a way to defend oneself without harming the opponent or aggressor. Overpowering the aggressor or opponent is the goal of winning a fight. Martial arts is a way to help maintain self-control and to deescalate a situation first and use self-defense techniques last. Most hollywood films portay martial arts as a violent way of hurting or killing someone to get what they want. It should be used as a last resort when it comes to defending oneself. Selling martial arts as a practice of looking invincible and gaining the upper-hand in any fight will spread more violence than peace.

A Real Altercation vs. A Controlled Altercation

A person that has trained in a controlled environment will not expect the same situation outside of the their training grounds. If a trained practitioner were to apply what they learned in a street altercation, they would find it difficult. One of the main reasons why it is difficult to apply the knowledge in a street altercation is the aggressors are not motionless. Additionally, the aggressors or opponents are not trained and have an awkward fighting style. A street altercation does not have rubber floor paddings or protective gear.

Self-Defense is not a Combat Sport

When taking a self-defense class, the techniques are set to buy enough time to prevent yourself from being harmed and to get as far as possible from the aggressor. A combat sport allows the students to not just defend themselves, but to also engage in a fight that lasts longer than a self-defense technique. One good example of combat sports is mixed-martial arts. This type of martial art combines multiple art forms and applies each technique that actually works on unorthodoxed aggressors. Finding the Ideal School for You

Before joining a martial arts school, it is recommended to do some research beforehand so that you can get a heads-up on what to expect. There are no schools that are the same because each instructor has a different way of teaching a martial art. Some can sell their martial art as a way to look invincible while some care more about money, which is why some prices are high and some classes offer more belt colors than usual. A tip to ensuring the instructor is legit is to ask for proof of their certification to see where they got their certifcation, when, and which martial art association they affiliate themselves with. One of the most common, but not so reliable, way of knowing which master or instructoris the best based on their body structure. I am not body shaming anyone, but having a smaller built intstructor is best. The reason is that they use their size to their advantage and not heavily relying on strength like most instructors that have muscular body. To get an in depth explanation, you can visit trifecta.com.

In Summary

In short, the belt color of an individual does not determine the capabilities of a person under any circumstances. Although trained for years, martial artists are limited to what they can do. How an instructor performs or runs a class will reflect on the students, so it is recommended to be careful when choosing a school and to do as much research as possible. The best way to avoid violence is simply to not be part of one and to not engage ourselves in any violence. Even the most skilled artists see defeat.
